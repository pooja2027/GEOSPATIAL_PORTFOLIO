Risk Score FormulaFormula
Risk Score = 0.35 × Population + 0.25 × Erosion + 0.20 × Rainfall + 0.20 × Coastline

All variables are min-max normalized across all 12 districts before weighting, ensuring comparability across different units. Final score is scaled 0–100.

Population Exposed — 35%
35%
Highest weight: human exposure drives real-world impact. A short but densely populated coast scores higher than a long unpopulated one.
Erosion Rate — 25%
25%
Physical coastline loss in m/year. Structural hazard representing long-term coastal degradation.
Rainfall Intensity — 20%
20%
Annual rainfall in mm. Precipitation drives flood triggering — particularly extreme monsoon events.
Coastline Length — 20%
20%
Total coastline in km. Longer exposure to marine hazards including storm surge and sea-level rise.
Study AreaSpatial

This dashboard analyzes coastal vulnerability across 12 districts in Tamil Nadu — from Chennai in the north to Kanyakumari in the south — selected because they represent the complete eastern and southern shoreline exposed to the Bay of Bengal and Indian Ocean. These districts collectively span 1,323 km of coastline and expose over 3,764 thousand people to coastal hazards.

VariablesData

Six variables were integrated: coastline length (km), population exposed to coastal flooding and erosion (thousands), historical shoreline erosion rate (m/year), annual flood event counts from 2010–2023, yearly rainfall totals (mm), and land-use composition (urban, agriculture, forest, wetland, other). These represent hazard, exposure, and vulnerability dimensions of coastal risk.

Visualization DesignDesign

Chart types were selected for analytical purpose, not aesthetic variety. Comparison charts (bar, lollipop, dumbbell) address district-level ranking. Trend charts (multi-line, area, calendar) capture temporal patterns. Distribution charts (histogram, box plot, dot plot) reveal spread and anomalies. Relationship charts (scatter, bubble, heatmap) expose correlations. Composition charts explain land-use differences. Multivariate charts present multiple factors simultaneously.

Data NoteSource

Data is synthetically generated based on published ranges from INCOIS Coastal Vulnerability Index reports and IMD district rainfall records for Tamil Nadu coastal districts (2010–2023). Values are realistic and internally consistent. Key sources: INCOIS CVI, IMD District Rainfall, Census of India 2011, NCSCM Coastal District Reports, data.gov.in flood records.
